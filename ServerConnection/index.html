Description

The purpose of this simulation is to show the growth of bacteria in different environments. Given lower temperatures the bacteria will have longer life spans, but their chance of consuming a "resource" and splitting is much smaller. The opposite is true for higher temperatures. Each split will create a new generation that keeps the color from the parent, but adds a variation value to it. That's why some of the bacteria will begin to change colors as the simulation goes on. This simulation represents the four phases of bacteria growth:


    1. Lag phase
    • The bacteria count is small and slow to start

    2. Exponential phase
    • The bacteria growth rate is exponential

    3. Stagnant phase
    • The bacteria growth rate levels out due to scarceness of resources

    4. Death phase
    • The bacteria count begins to drop because of the lack of resources to continue splitting

Adjusting the starting resource value to increase or decrease the total number of bacteria.


Adjusting the minutes will also increase the lifespan of the bacteria, but only so the simulation will still function.


Stepping is there to show the rapid growth of the bacteria. However, it does not change the life of any bacteria made. So after resuming all bacteria will have a full lifespan still.


Caution: This simulation does not limit the values in any way except to keep any non-numbers from being inputted. Because of this there could be a serious lag at higher bacteria counts for weak computers.
